MARIAM MAKEBA

She was one of the most extraordinary artists of the twentieth century, an icon of South African music. She was the consummate musical storyteller, a griot as she would come to learn, who used her remarkable talent to create a legacy of liberation, empowerment, passion, and love through a magnificent body of works. She was who the world would come to know as Mama Africa.

Early Life

Born Zenzile Mariam Makeba on March 4, 1932, in the black township of Prospect, near Johannesburg, Mariam Makeba. Her Swazi mother, Christina Makeba, was a sangoma,or traditional healer, and a domestic worker. Her Xhosa father, Caswell Makeba, was a teacher, he died when she was six years old.

Civil Rights Singer

Makeba began her professional musical career with the Cuban Brothers, a South African all-male close harmony group, with whom she sang covers of popular American songs. soon afterwards, at the age of 21, she joined a jazz group, the Manhattan Brothers, who sang a mixture of South African songs and pieces from popular African-American groups.Makeba was the only woman in the group. With the Manhattan Brothers she recorded her first hit, "Laku Tshoni Ilanga", in 1953, and developed a national reputation as a musician. In 1956 she joined a new all-woman group, the Skylarks, singing a blend of jazz and traditional South African melodies.Formed by Gallotone Records, the group was also known as the Sunbeams

Legacy

In her years,Mariam Makeba battled with health problems. She was battling breast cancer, She died on 9 November 2008 aged 76 Castel Volturno, Italy.
While she may be gone, Mariam left a lasting impression on the world of music. She sang to share her truth, and her work still resonates with great emotion and power.
